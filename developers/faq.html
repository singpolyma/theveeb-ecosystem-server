So you want to distribute your applications?

Isn't cross-platform development hard?

That really depends on what kind of application you're building. There are a number of libraries and frameworks out there that can help you build your software in a more portable fashion. Check out our tips on cross-platform development.

What kind of guarantees do I have from you?

Here at The Veeb, we know that developers are what make our services useful to consumers. Without applications to distribute, there is no point in having a distribution system. With that in mind, we make the following commitments:

  • We will not distribute your software to any person or organisation that has not paid a minimum of your asking price.
  • We will not allow other developers to use your name, logo, or other material in their applications without your permission.
  • We will not make it ridiculously hard to get your money. If you've made money, then you're entitled to have it.

What if I need a library you don't have?

The Veeb Ecosystem handles installing all dependency libraries of your application, so that you don't have to. If we do not currently have the libraries you need, we will get them. We need to be able to redistribute the libraries, however, so some proprietary libraries may not be options. If you can convince the library author to allow us to distribute it, then we should be able to work something out. In addition, we cannot usually port proprietary libraries to new platforms and architectures as we choose to add support for them, so they should be avoided if at all possible.

How much of the money do I get?

Currently we are sharing revenue 70/30 with developers. That is, you get 70% of the revenue from the sale of any of your applications.

How much does it cost to distribute with The Veeb Ecosystem?

Getting your application into The Veeb Ecosystem costs nothing.

What do these content licenses mean?

These are licenses that you can choose to apply to the "content" in your application. Content is graphics, sound, and anything else that is not source code. Creative Commons Attribution
People can do anything with your content, as long as they give you credit.
Creative Commons Attribution ShareAlike
People can do anything with your content, as long as they give you credit and distribute any work they base on yours under the same license.
Creative Commons Attribution NonCommercial ShareAlike
People can do anything with your content, as long as they give you credit and distribute any work they base on yours under the same license and do not use it for "commercial purposes".
Creative Commons Attribution NonCommercial NoDerivatives
People can do anything with your content, as long as they give you credit, do not use the content for "commercial purposes", and do not base any new work on yours.
All Right Reserved
People can only use the content in compliance with standard copyright law. They cannot redistribute it or base new work on it.

What do these source licenses mean?

These are licenses that you can choose to apply to the source code of your application. By default we offer support for the following licenses. (Disclaimer: these are not complete or legal descriptions of the licenses, only informal summaries. None of these licenses affect normal fair use or fair dealings or permit otherwise criminal activity.) In all cases, users must give you credit for your work.

Artistic License 2.0
People may redistribute the source. They may distribute modifications if they clearly document them and make such modification available to you for re-inclusion if you wish.
ISC License
People may redistribute the source and/or modifications to the source.
CDDL
People may redistribute the source. They may redistribute modifications if they license their work under the CDDL. The source can be mixed with source under other licenses (with the exception of the GPL) easily.
GPL v3
People may redistribute the source. They may redistribute modifications if they license their work under the GPL. The source cannot be mixed with source of other licenses easily. New projects should use v3, the latest version of the GPL, although we will allow existing projects under v2 to maintain their license and distribute with us without any problem.
